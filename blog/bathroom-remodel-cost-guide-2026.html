Bathroom Remodel Cost Guide 2026: What You'll Really Pay

Updated March 2026 · 10 min read

The average bathroom remodel in 2026 costs between $8,000 and $35,000 — but that range is almost useless without context. A master bath gut renovation in Manhattan costs dramatically more than a half-bath refresh in rural Tennessee. This guide breaks down actual 2026 costs by project type, region, and quality level, so you know exactly what to budget before you pick up the phone.

Bottom line up front: Budget remodels run $6,000–$12,000. Mid-range hits $15,000–$28,000. Full luxury renovations start at $35,000 and go well past $80,000 for a large primary bath. Location multiplies costs by up to 1.6x in high-cost cities.

What Drives Costs Up (and What Keeps Them Down)

Factors that increase your bathroom remodel cost

  • Moving plumbing: Relocating a toilet, tub, or shower drain can add $1,500–$5,000+ just for the plumbing work. Keep fixtures in the same general location when possible.
  • Electrical upgrades: Adding GFCI outlets, heated floors, or a new ventilation fan circuit typically costs $500–$2,500 depending on panel capacity.
  • Custom tile work: Intricate patterns, large-format tiles, or custom shower niches add $15–$40/sqft in labor vs. $6–$10 for standard installation.
  • Structural issues: Water damage behind walls or rotted subfloor discovered during demo can add $2,000–$10,000 to the project unexpectedly.
  • High-end fixtures: A Kohler or Toto luxury toilet costs $800–$2,000 vs. $150–$400 for a standard unit. Rainfall showers and custom vanities have similar price gaps.

Ways to save on your bathroom remodel

  • Keep plumbing in place: This single decision can save $2,000–$5,000.
  • Use stock vanities: IKEA SEKTION, Home Depot, or Wayfair stock vanities cost $200–$1,200. Custom cabinetry runs $3,000–$8,000+.
  • Buy your own fixtures: Contractor markup on fixtures runs 20–40%. Buy directly from Amazon or Home Depot and supply them to your contractor.
  • Tile the shower, not the floor: High-end tile in the shower has more visual impact per dollar than expensive floor tile.
  • Get 3+ bids: Labor quotes can vary 30–50% for identical work. Always get multiple bids.

Budget vs. Mid-Range vs. Premium: What's the Difference?

Budget bathroom remodel ($6,000–$12,000)

A budget remodel doesn't mean ugly — it means strategic. You're keeping the layout, using stock products, and doing some DIY. Typical budget remodel: repaint walls, replace vanity with a stock unit ($300–$600), new toilet ($200–$400), new light fixture, re-caulk the tub, and replace the faucet. Result: a completely refreshed bathroom for under $10,000.

Mid-range bathroom remodel ($15,000–$28,000)

The sweet spot for most homeowners. This level allows you to retile the shower, replace the flooring, install a new tub or walk-in shower with a glass door, add a double vanity, and upgrade all fixtures. You can get quality without custom pricing.

Premium/luxury bathroom remodel ($35,000+)

At this level, everything is custom. Walk-in shower with custom tile and multiple showerheads, freestanding soaking tub, heated floors, custom-built vanity with quartz or marble countertop, smart lighting, and high-end brands throughout.

Permits: Don't Skip This Step

Most full bathroom remodels require permits for plumbing and electrical work. Costs vary: $100–$500 in most jurisdictions. Skipping permits creates serious problems when you sell — buyers' inspectors will find unpermitted work, which can kill a deal or require expensive remediation. Always pull permits for structural, plumbing, and electrical work.

Timeline: How Long Does a Bathroom Remodel Take?

  • Cosmetic refresh (paint, fixtures): 1–3 days
  • Partial remodel (vanity, toilet, floor): 1–2 weeks
  • Full remodel (gut to finish): 3–6 weeks
  • Primary bath luxury remodel: 6–10 weeks
Pro tip: The biggest causes of timeline delays are (1) custom product lead times — order fixtures and tile before demo begins, (2) permit processing — apply 2–4 weeks before your start date, and (3) discovering hidden water damage that wasn't in the original scope.

ROI: Does a Bathroom Remodel Add Home Value?

According to Remodeling Magazine's Cost vs. Value report, a mid-range bathroom remodel recoups about 60–70% of its cost at resale. A primary suite addition recoups 50–60%. This means remodeling primarily for resale value is usually not financially optimal — but if you're staying in the home for 5+ years, the value you get from daily use justifies the investment.

That said, an outdated or dysfunctional bathroom actively hurts resale. Buyers discount heavily for dated bathrooms — bringing one to current standards often pays back close to 100% of cost.
